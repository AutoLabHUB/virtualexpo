1. Platform overview

A modern virtual expo that can start lean and grow into a full-featured digital venue with meeting scheduler, exhibitor portals, and automation.

The goal is to create a virtual expo experience that feels dynamic and premium, while staying manageable for your team behind the scenes. The platform can start with a focused core (e.g. one main event or a showcase) and expand into multiple expos, tiers of exhibitors, and on-demand content.

Core pillars of the solution

  • Virtual Expo Hall & Lobby: immersive layout for exhibitors, sessions, and navigation.
  • Meeting Scheduler: attendees (and admins) can book sessions with exhibitors based on real-time availability.
  • Exhibitor Portals: exhibitors can manage their booth, content, meeting availability, and incoming leads.
  • Session Hosting: support for live webinars, pre-recorded content, and hybrid formats.
  • Admin Console: configuration of events, exhibitors, tickets, sponsorship tiers, and content.

Technical approach (high level)

  • Modern web front-end built for performance and UX (e.g. Next.js / React).
  • Secure backend for expo data, exhibitors, and bookings (e.g. Supabase / Postgres + server-side logic).
  • Integration-ready for external tools (Outlook / Gmail calendar sync, payment providers, email, etc.).
  • Deployed on scalable infrastructure with CDN and SSL by default.

2. Key features & user flows

This section summarises the main modules for attendees, exhibitors, and admins, including the meeting scheduler and session hosting.

Attendee experience

  • Expo Lobby & Navigation: branded landing page and immersive hall views with category filters.
  • Exhibitor Discovery: search & filters (industry, category, sponsorship level, tags).
  • Meeting Scheduler:
    • Attendees can request meetings with specific exhibitors.
    • Real-time availability based on exhibitor calendars (Outlook / Gmail sync) or schedules defined in the app.
    • Confirmations and calendar invites sent via email.
  • Session Access: join live seminars, panel discussions, and workshops directly from session cards.
  • Personal Agenda: view upcoming meetings, sessions, and reminders in one place.

Exhibitor experience

  • Exhibitor Dashboard: manage booth info, logo, media, and key links.
  • Meeting availability:
    • Connect Outlook or Gmail calendar for availability sync; or
    • Manually define time slots inside the app if they prefer not to connect their calendar.
    • View, accept, or reschedule meeting requests.
  • Session hosting:
    • Host live webinars via integrated providers (e.g. Zoom / embedded webinar tools).
    • Upload pre-recorded videos for on-demand content.
    • Attach resources: PDFs, links, contact forms.
  • Lead Management: see who visited their booth, attended sessions, and requested meetings.

Admin & organiser tools

  • Configure events, expos, themes, and branding.
  • Set up exhibitor tiers, packages, and sponsorship.
  • Approve and onboard exhibitors (self-service onboarding possible in later phases).
  • Manage sessions, speakers, and tracks (seminars, panels, workshops, networking lounges).
  • Control meeting rules:
    • Max meetings per exhibitor per day.
    • Buffer times, durations, and time windows.
    • Whether attendees book directly or request approval.
  • Automation hooks for:
    • Booking confirmations and reminders.
    • Post-session follow-up emails to attendees / exhibitors.
    • Billing summaries and invoices by exhibitor.

Future optional extensions

  • Gamification (leaderboards, check-in streaks).
  • AI-driven exhibitor recommendations for attendees.
  • Multi-event / multi-venue support and archives.
  • Sponsor analytics dashboards.
  • White-labeling for partners or franchises.
★
The platform is designed to start at the level that makes sense for your first release, with a clear roadmap for more advanced modules as the expo grows.

3. Phased delivery

We can launch this in stages – starting lean, then layering on deeper automation and analytics as you validate demand.

Phase 1 – Core expo & meetings (MVP)

  • Branded lobby + expo hall layout.
  • Exhibitor profiles with core information and media.
  • Basic meeting scheduler:
    • Admin-configured meeting slots per exhibitor; or
    • Simple calendar sync (initial limited integration)
  • Basic admin console for events, exhibitors, and meeting rules.
  • Email notifications for confirmed bookings & cancellations.

Phase 2 – Exhibitor portals & session hosting

  • Self-service exhibitor login and booth management.
  • Outlook / Gmail availability sync for deeper integration (if not included in Phase 1).
  • Webinar / session hosting flows.
  • Attendee agenda view (meetings, sessions, reminders).
  • Initial analytics for admins and exhibitors.

Subsequent phases can focus on scaling, sponsorship enhancements, and automation such as billing summaries per exhibitor, post-event engagement, and repeat expo cloning.
